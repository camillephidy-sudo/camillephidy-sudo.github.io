## Abstract

Published in *Remote Sensing Technology and Application* (2022, **37(6): 1427–1436**), this study combines **numerical simulation** and **controlled ground experiments** to characterize how **undulating topography** modulates microwave radiation—establishing the experimental basis for later topographic correction and plateau-scale remote sensing work.

## Authors

T. Liu, S. Zhao, **D. Chen** (corresponding author), et al.

## Objectives

- Quantify sensitivity of microwave observations to **surface roughness** and **slope variability**.
- Validate simulated radiative responses against **field measurements** on structured undulating targets.
- Provide evidence for correction schemes in non-flat landscapes where plane-surface assumptions fail.

## Methods

| Track | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Simulation** | Forward modeling of geometry-driven microwave response on undulating surfaces |
| **Field campaign** | Ground-based measurements on controlled test surfaces with known relief |
| **Analysis** | Comparative assessment linking model predictions to observed radiation patterns |

## Findings

- Undulating relief produces systematic, predictable distortions in microwave signatures.
- Simulation–experiment agreement supports **physics-based** rather than purely statistical corrections.
- Results inform sensor configuration and preprocessing for rugged-terrain campaigns.
