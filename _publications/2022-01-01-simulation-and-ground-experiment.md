This early-career study combines **numerical simulation** and **controlled ground experiments** to quantify how **undulating topography** alters microwave radiation signatures.

## Objectives

- Characterize sensitivity of observed microwave signals to surface roughness and slope variability.
- Validate simulated radiative behavior against field measurements on structured undulating targets.
- Provide experimental evidence for correction and inversion schemes in non-flat landscapes.

## Methods

- Forward/simulation experiments exploring geometry-driven microwave response.
- Ground-based measurement campaigns on undulating test surfaces.
- Comparative analysis linking model outputs to observed radiation patterns.

## Role in my research trajectory

The paper anchors my later **topographic correction (IEEE J-STARS, 2023)** and **mountain/plateau field programs**, forming the experimental foundation for physics-aware microwave remote sensing rather than treating terrain as a nuisance residual.
