This paper introduces a **vegetation moisture inversion framework** that couples the **Water Cloud Model (WCM)** with **Bayesian inference** to retrieve canopy water state from microwave remote sensing.

## Motivation

Classical WCM inversions often treat noise and parameter uncertainty implicitly, which limits stability across seasons and crop types. A probabilistic formulation makes retrievals more interpretable and better suited to operational monitoring.

## Approach

- Forward modeling with WCM linking vegetation water content to backscatter.
- Bayesian updating of vegetation and soil moisture parameters from multi-temporal Sentinel-1–class observations.
- Regularization and prior structure to reduce ill-posedness in dense vegetation scenes.

## Contributions

- A novel inversion algorithm unifying WCM physics with Bayesian estimation.
- Improved robustness for vegetation moisture mapping under variable agricultural conditions.
- A pathway toward **uncertainty-aware** vegetation monitoring compatible with downstream risk and irrigation decision support.

## Relevance

This work complements my broader portfolio on **microwave inversion**, **GOESSD salinity monitoring**, and **IGARSS crop water-content studies**, emphasizing physics-consistent, statistically principled retrieval rather than black-box regression alone.
