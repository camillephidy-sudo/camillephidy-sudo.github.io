## Abstract

Published in **IEEE J-STARS** (vol. 16, pp. 9272–9285), this paper develops an **empirical topographic correction model** for microwave radiation over **mountainous terrain**, where slope, aspect, and local incidence angle systematically bias brightness temperature and backscatter products.

## Authors

S. Zhao, T. Liu, L. Chai, P. Wang, **D. Chen** (corresponding author), T. Zhang

## Scientific context

Microwave retrievals of soil moisture, freeze–thaw state, and vegetation stress assume relatively flat effective surfaces. In relief terrain:

- **Geometric distortion** modulates incidence angle and path length.
- **Relief shadows and layover** (active systems) create spatially structured artifacts.
- Uncorrected stacks produce **false temporal trends** mistaken for hydrological or cryospheric change.

## Model overview

1. Relate terrain geometry (slope, aspect, elevation) to observed microwave radiation characteristics.
2. Estimate empirical correction factors for mountainous land surfaces.
3. Normalize multi-temporal observations before inversion or anomaly detection.
