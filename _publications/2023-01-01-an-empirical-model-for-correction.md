Published in **IEEE J-STARS**, this paper addresses a persistent error source in passive/active microwave products over **rugged topography**: unresolved topographic modulation of emitted and scattered radiation.

## Background

In mountainous areas, slope, aspect, and local incidence angle distort apparent microwave signals, biasing soil moisture, freeze–thaw, and vegetation retrievals if left uncorrected.

## Model

- Derives an **empirical topographic correction** relating terrain geometry to observed microwave radiation characteristics.
- Targets mountainous land surfaces where radiative transfer assumptions break down at coarse resolution.
- Enables fairer comparison of multi-temporal stacks prior to inversion or anomaly detection.

## Significance

Topographic normalization is a prerequisite for regional algorithms—including later **GOESSD salinity monitoring** and **high-plateau field studies**—where relief-driven artifacts would otherwise masquerade as environmental change.

## Takeaway

The work establishes a practical correction layer in my microwave remote sensing toolchain, supporting scientifically defensible analysis in complex terrain such as the Tibetan Plateau and irrigated basin margins.
