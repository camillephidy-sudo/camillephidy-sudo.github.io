Teaching assistant for **Microwave Remote Sensing and LiDAR**, an advanced undergraduate compulsory course at Beijing Normal University (Sep 2021 – Jun 2024).

## Course scope

- Fundamentals of **microwave remote sensing** (wavelengths, scattering mechanisms, platform geometry)
- **Passive and active** systems, including radiometry and radar concepts
- **LiDAR** acquisition, point-cloud processing, and integration with raster EO workflows
- Links between sensor physics and land-surface applications (moisture, vegetation structure, terrain)

## Responsibilities

- Supported lectures and **laboratory sessions** on SAR/LiDAR data handling
- Mentored students on **preprocessing, visualization, and interpretation** of microwave and LiDAR products
- Assisted with assignment design feedback, grading, and office-hour consultations
- Bridged course material to research practice in **Sentinel-1** and topographic remote sensing

## Relevance to my research

This role reinforced the pedagogical foundation behind my later work on **microwave inversion**, **topographic correction (IEEE J-STARS)**, and **field-validated** remote sensing pipelines.
