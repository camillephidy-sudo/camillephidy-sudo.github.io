## Expedition Conclusion: July 22 - August 11, 2022

The Second Tibetan Plateau Comprehensive Scientific Expedition's freeze-thaw research subproject completed fieldwork on August 11, 2022, covering Gangcha County and Qilian County within Qilian Mountain National Park (Qinghai section). This 21-day intensive field campaign investigated the cascading impacts of intensifying freeze-thaw processes on soil systems and ecosystem health.

## Critical Research Context

Recent decades have witnessed dramatic intensification of freeze-thaw cycles across the Tibetan Plateau due to global warming and anthropogenic climate change. This phenomenon poses **severe challenges to soil system health** on the plateau, necessitating comprehensive scientific investigation.

## Expedition Scope and Data Collection

### Geographic Coverage
- Total route distance: approximately 5,000 km round-trip
- Study regions: Gangcha and Qilian County areas (Qinghai Province)
- Elevation range: 3,500-4,500 m above sea level
- Focus points: 19 key sampling locations

### Data Collected
- **Soil samples**: Collected from control and freeze-thaw disturbed areas across multiple sites
- **Water samples**: Isotope samples from tributary streams, major rivers, and thermokarst lakes
- **Aerial documentation**: High-resolution UAV imagery of key landforms
- **In-situ measurements**: Permafrost temperature, soil moisture, ground ice content

## Major Findings and Observations

### 1. Thermokarst Slump Dynamics
Early-stage thermokarst slumping emerged as a critical geomorphological process, with direct impacts on soil profile exposure and water erosion. Observable progression toward thermokarst gully formation suggests future landscape transformation.

### 2. Permafrost Mound Complexity
Frost-heave hillocks and incipient thermokarst lakes create spatially heterogeneous freeze-thaw intensity gradients, enabling comparative investigation of soil quality across the disturbance gradient.

### 3. Hydrological Integration
Water isotope analysis reveals complex linkages between freeze-thaw processes and regional hydrological cycles. Subsurface ice melt contributions to river-source wetlands indicate non-trivial cryospheric-hydrospheric coupling.

### 4. Thermokarst Lake Expansion
Thermokarst lake group complexes demonstrate progressive landscape transformation, with expanding lakes indicative of accelerating permafrost degradation rates.

## Soil Quality and Ecosystem Implications

The expedition addressed four critical research objectives:

1. **Freeze-thaw intensity assessment**: Quantitative characterization across periglacial landscape types
2. **Soil structural impacts**: Understanding changes in soil aggregation, porosity, and mechanical properties
3. **Microbial community shifts**: Documenting soil biological responses to modified freeze-thaw regimes
4. **Carbon cycle modifications**: Assessing soil respiration and carbon emission changes

## Broader Significance

This research contributes to the **"Ecosystems and Ecological Security"** component of the Second Tibetan Plateau Comprehensive Scientific Expedition, specifically addressing soil quality changes under climate stress. Findings provide critical baseline data for:

- Long-term monitoring of cryospheric-ecosystem interactions
- Climate model validation and refinement
- Predictive frameworks for ecosystem degradation risk
- Policy-informed land management strategies in high-altitude regions

## Future Research Directions

Laboratory analysis of collected samples will quantify:
- Soil physicochemical properties (texture, organic matter, nutrient content)
- Biological indicators (microbial biomass, enzyme activity)
- Hydrothermal regime modifications
- Carbon storage and flux dynamics

Multi-year monitoring of established field sites will enable assessment of temporal dynamics and climate sensitivity of Tibetan Plateau periglacial ecosystems.

## Expedition Team Contributions

The success of this intensive field campaign reflects collaborative effort from:
- Graduate researchers and field team members
- Senior researchers and technical staff
- UAV documentation and remote sensing specialists
- Laboratory analysis teams

This work represents a critical step toward comprehensive understanding of freeze-thaw process impacts on Tibetan Plateau ecosystem resilience in the face of ongoing climate change.
