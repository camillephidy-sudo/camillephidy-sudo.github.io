## Huazhaizi Field Site

**Huazhaizi** oasis station anchors the heterogeneous cropland–desert transition zone of the middle Heihe River Basin. During the **September 22, 2019** ET/SM synthesis workshop, our team finalized radiometer configurations and consolidated ground observations from the month-long **Heihe integrated remote sensing experiment**.

## RPG Microwave Radiometer System

Huazhaizi hosts **RPG (Radiometer Physics GmbH)** microwave systems integrated with rail-mounted platforms for repeatable zenith/nadir sampling. Red-aperture auxiliary sensors and GPS time synchronization ensure traceable brightness temperature records for **soil-moisture retrieval validation**.

## Scientific Outcomes (2019 Field Season)

Ground campaigns directly supported:

- **Soil moisture product disaggregation** (Random Forest, 0.25° → 0.01°) validated at six superstations including Daman and Huazhaizi
- **ET product algorithm benchmarking** against eddy-covariance towers
- Integration with the **Qilian Mountains water–ecosystem–economy observation system** (24 upgraded stations; XDA20100101)

## Personal Role

I participated in **sensor excavation/installation**, **depth-verified soil profiling**, **radiometer field operations**, and **data QA**—building the field credibility behind my subsequent microwave inversion publications ([IGARSS 2024](/publication/2024-01-01-retrieving-corn-gravimetric-water-content/), [Journal of Hydrology under review](/publication/2026-01-01-sar-soil-moisture-bayesian-wcm-hydrology/)) and the **[GOESSD monitoring system](/patent/2023-soil-salinization-detection-monitoring-system/)**.

## Closing Remarks

The 2019 Heihe upgrade demonstrated that authentic remote sensing products require **traceable in-situ networks**, not desktop algorithms alone. Multi-depth soil pits, tower fluxes, and RPG radiometry formed a closed validation loop that continues to inform my work on **Bayesian–WCM soil moisture retrieval** and regional ecohydrological monitoring.
