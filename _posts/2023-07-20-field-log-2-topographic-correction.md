## Microwave Radiation Anomalies

During the July phase of fieldwork, we observed systematic biases in microwave radiometric measurements along steep (>30°) slope aspects. These anomalies ranged from -8 K to +12 K brightness temperature variations compared to flat terrain predictions.

## Empirical Model Development

Our team developed a novel empirical model incorporating:
- Local incidence angle calculations from high-resolution DEMs (2m resolution)
- Slope and aspect-dependent emissivity corrections
- Vegetation-topography interaction parameters

The model achieved mean absolute error (MAE) of 0.63 K across validation sites, representing a 76% improvement over conventional methods.

## Ground Calibration Measurements

Twelve calibration points were established on slopes of 15°, 25°, and 38° angles. Simultaneous in-situ radiometric and thermal data collection enabled direct empirical validation of topographic correction factors.

## Expedition Impact

This work has direct applications for improving satellite-based soil moisture estimates in mountainous regions globally, particularly relevant for the Himalayas and other high-relief terrain.
