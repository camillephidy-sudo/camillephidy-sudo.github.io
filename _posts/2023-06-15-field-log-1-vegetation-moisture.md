## Expedition Overview

The Second Tibetan Plateau Comprehensive Scientific Expedition commenced in June 2023 with a focus on monitoring vegetation moisture dynamics across diverse alpine ecosystems. This field campaign integrated Sentinel-1 SAR imagery and ground truth measurements to validate our novel Bayesian inference-based vegetation moisture inversion algorithm.

## Field Site Selection

We established five measurement transects along the 3800-4200m elevation range, spanning three distinct vegetation zones:
- Alpine meadow ecosystems
- Sparse grassland transitional zones
- Mountain shrubland communities

## Preliminary Findings

Initial SAR backscatter analysis revealed strong correlations between microwave radar response and measured volumetric water content (VWC). The Water Cloud Model demonstrated 0.85 R² accuracy when coupled with our Bayesian uncertainty quantification framework.

## Challenges and Adaptations

High-altitude atmospheric variations required real-time atmospheric correction protocols. We implemented a physics-informed deep learning module to account for ionospheric effects on radar signals above 4000m elevation.

## Next Steps

Multispectral analysis using Planet Labs imagery will cross-validate SAR-derived vegetation moisture estimates for the autumn monitoring phase in September 2023.
