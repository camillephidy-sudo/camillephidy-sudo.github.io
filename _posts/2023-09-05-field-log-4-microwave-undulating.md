## Surface Roughness Experiments

September fieldwork concentrated on characterizing microwave radiation signatures across natural undulating surfaces with varying roughness parameters. This represents a critical gap in current microwave radiative transfer models.

## Experimental Design

We selected 15 study plots representing a gradient of surface roughness:
- Smooth ice/snow (correlation length: 1-3cm)
- Smooth mineral soil (correlation length: 2-5cm)
- Rough alpine vegetation (correlation length: 5-15cm)
- Very rough scree/talus slopes (correlation length: 15-50cm)

## Ground Truth Collection

Multi-frequency microwave measurements (L, C, X-band) were synchronized with:
- High-resolution photogrammetry-derived surface roughness metrics
- Permittivity measurements via ground-penetrating radar
- Soil moisture profiles (0-20cm depth)

## Key Discoveries

Our simulations using FEKO electromagnetic solver and in-situ validation revealed that conventional roughness parameters (RMS height, correlation length) are insufficient for mountainous terrain. We developed a novel topographic roughness index (TRI) that incorporates slope-aspect coupling.

## Publication Significance

These findings will refine operational soil moisture retrieval algorithms used by ESA Sentinel-1 and JAXA ALOS-2 satellites serving millions of users globally.
