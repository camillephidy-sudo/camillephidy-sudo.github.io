## Observing Climate Resilience

The August expedition phase focused on quantifying surface albedo changes and vegetation phenology shifts across snow-covered and snow-free areas of the Tibetan Plateau. These observations are critical for understanding climate-ecosystem feedback mechanisms.

## Spectral Measurements

Using hyperspectral field radiometers, we collected surface reflectance data across 400-2500nm wavelength range at 30 locations:
- Alpine snow/ice zones (albedo: 0.72-0.89)
- Bare rock and scree (albedo: 0.15-0.35)
- Vegetated areas (albedo: 0.18-0.32)

## Large Vision Models Integration

We tested a multimodal Earth Observation Foundation Model to classify subpixel-scale ecosystem types from high-resolution Planet Labs imagery. The model achieved 91% accuracy in distinguishing vegetation density classes, outperforming traditional spectral indices.

## Carbon Cycle Implications

Early results suggest a 2-3% annual increase in exposed rock surfaces due to glacier retreat, potentially amplifying surface heating through reduced albedo—a critical positive feedback mechanism identified in climate models.

## Ongoing Analysis

We are integrating these field observations with 20 years of Landsat data to establish long-term albedo trends and validate climate model projections.
